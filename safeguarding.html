Safeguarding Policy

1. Policy Statement

We are fully committed to safeguarding and promoting the welfare of all children and young people. Every child has the right to feel safe and protected. Our policies ensure a secure, inclusive, and supportive environment at all times.

3. Who This Policy Applies To

  • Children and young people under 18 involved in the academy
  • All coaches, staff, and volunteers
  • Parents, carers, and guardians

4. Our Safeguarding Principles

  • Child welfare is paramount
  • All children are treated with dignity and respect
  • Concerns are acted upon promptly and seriously
  • Safeguarding is everyone’s responsibility
  • We promote a safe, empowering culture

5. Recognising Abuse

Our team is trained to identify and respond to signs of:

  • Physical abuse
  • Emotional abuse
  • Sexual abuse
  • Neglect
  • Bullying and cyberbullying

6. Code of Conduct

  • Put children’s welfare first
  • Maintain professional boundaries
  • Avoid unnecessary physical contact
  • Never tolerate abuse or discrimination
  • Report concerns without delay

7. Reporting Concerns

If you have concerns about a child’s welfare:

  1. Notify the Lead or Deputy Safeguarding Officer immediately
  2. If urgent, call 999 or contact children’s services

8. Recruitment and Training

  • Enhanced DBS checks
  • Reference checks
  • Ongoing safeguarding training
  • Professional development

9. Online Safety

  • Professional and appropriate digital communication
  • Use of children’s images only with written consent
  • Monitored digital platforms

10. Supervision and Ratios

  • Safe adult-to-child ratios
  • No 1:1 situations unless logged
  • Respectful changing and toilet supervision
  • Safe drop-off and pick-up policies

11. Review and Updates

This policy is reviewed annually or in line with legislative changes.
