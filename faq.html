Frequently Asked Questions

General Information

What age can my child start at the academy?

Children can join from age 10 upwards. We group sessions by age and ability to ensure everyone feels confident and supported.

Can girls join the academy?

Yes! Our academy welcomes both boys and girls. We are fully inclusive and encourage all young people to get involved, build skills, and grow in confidence.

Does my child need previous boxing experience?

No experience is required! Our program is designed for complete beginners through to advanced young boxers.

Is the academy safe?

Absolutely. All coaches are DBS-checked, qualified, and trained in safeguarding and first aid. Safety gear is used appropriately and our sessions are designed to build skills before contact is introduced.

Session Information

What should my child wear and bring?

  • Comfortable sportswear (shorts/leggings and t-shirt)
  • Trainers with good grip
  • A bottle of water
  • Boxing gloves and hand wraps (we can advise on purchasing or provide starter kits)

What happens in a typical session?

  • Warm-up with light cardio, mobility drills, and dynamic stretches
  • Skill development: stance, guard, footwork, punches, defense
  • Conditioning: fitness circuits and boxing drills
  • Controlled partner work (non-contact for beginners)
  • Cool-down and stretching

Booking and Costs

How much does it cost?

  • Monthly Membership: £45 (covers weekly structured sessions led by qualified coaches)
  • Enrolment Fee: £20 per level (covers admin, progress tracking, and resources)
  • Assessment/Grading Fee: £30 every 3 months (covers skills assessment and certificate)

Additional costs for kit may apply (see Welcome Pack for details).

How do assessments work?

Children progress through skill levels every 3 months. Assessments are designed to be supportive and confidence-building. Each child is assessed individually.

Other Information

Do you offer competitive boxing?

Yes. Once children have developed the necessary skills, they may be invited to become carded and enter competitions. We’ll discuss this with you when your child is ready.

Can I stay and watch?

Yes – especially for younger children. However, we may occasionally ask parents to watch from the viewing area to encourage independence and focus.

What happens if my child misses a session?

We understand that things come up. If you let us know in advance, we’ll try to offer catch-up options where possible.

How do we stay in touch?

  • Email updates
  • Text alerts for important reminders
  • Our official website and Gymcatch platform
  • Social media channels
